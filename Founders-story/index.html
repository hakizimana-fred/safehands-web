Who Is Batte Nolbert?

Young Batte Nolbert

BE A PENCIL GOD USES TO WRITE A MESSAGE OF HOPE, LOVE IN LIVES OF PEOPLE. This self quote always makes me smile and pushes me to creating a sustainable impact in the lives of those in need and creating opportunities for them for a quality life.

My name is Batte Nolbert am the founder of Safe hands children foundation the creation of this charity was an inspiration drawn from my life. I was born on 17th October 1996 in a small village of Busujju Wakiso district Uganda Africa although we spent our lives moving to different places with my mother hoping to find settled life. I never witnessed the happy years between my mother and father this is because during the prenatal stage of my mother I was abandoned by my father before entering the world, I was left without a father Figure apart from God who has been my heavenly Father. I was raised by a single mother who always wished nothing but a happy life for me although it was hard to achieve. I lived in a life in case we failed to get what to eat in the night my mother would use stories to make me forget that my stomach was empty because they were fun although my stomach still made a lot of noise in the night you can imagine that sound we used to have silent nights of Hunger. I used to be worried of the night and day at the same time.

At some point of my life I lost faith in Christ and become a victim of social pressure this made me lose sight of my spiritual way because of the unchanging poor conditions and spiritual impatience I was living in which was filled with a lot of social isolation, illness tendencies, and financial hardship. My mother struggled to bring me back on the track of Christ that was the most terrifying period of my life. My life gradually changed and I later turned back to God and slowly discovered my true purpose as time passed.

My mother's greatest sacrifice was to see me complete my studies. During my life that I won't regret because God gave it meaning, I witnessed an environment where child exploration is the one route of survival, bullying is a normal activity done on impoverished children, a girl child is an object and underestimated, boy child, is not given guidance on how to deal with their social development leading to a lot of vices like drug abuse, robbery, and defilement with the rapid decline in survivorship of children and families in communities.

In 2016, due to the financial struggle involving loans and low income that affected my academics and survival I wasn't going to get a chance to continue to the university to complete my bachelor of Industrial-organizational psychology. My Heavenly Father was so good to me as always, He did not leave me in the time of great need. I met a good Samaritan lady from the USA on Instagram who helped me get back on the academic track to complete my degree she helped my family and also experience some new things I dreamt of like having a good meal on Christmas and celebrate my first birthday on October 17th. The friendship inspired me to use the social media platform to start the quest of my purpose of helping my kind the orphans, vulnerable, and needy children to create a social sustainable impact in communities.

Praise be to the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, the Father of compassion and the God of all comfort, who comforts us in all our troubles so that we can comfort those in any trouble with the comfort we receive from God. 2Corinthians 1:3-4. I began to realize that from my own experience as a child who grew under survival anxiety it had sensitized me and prepared me to reach out and care for other orphans. All the pain, the fear, and dread at night, the deprivation I see in the eyes of the poor and orphans, I felt before them. My heart could never have gone out to them as it does today, due to my experience. My Heavenly Father would use My past pain and the comfort He gave me, to offer comfort to children. God works all things together for good to those who love Him.

I finally graduated from Makerere with Bachelor of industrial-organizational psychology. I decided to embark on a faith journey to care for poor and orphaned children. I prayerfully considered this decision and felt like this is what God was calling for my life purpose. It has become one of my most rewarding jobs spiritually and mentally I could ever imagine.

In 2017 teamed with my Friend Faiso who always wanted to help orphans. I started doing tasks like carrying water, brickmaking and all the income was directed to the orphanage which had seven children. In a tiny room where I lived sleeping on the floor safe hands foundation was created believing that when children are in safe hands as they grow in life, they can have an improved social, mental, spiritual and financial development in life.

Due to my grassroots knowledge of the community and extended assistance from Faiso we began visiting some children that I knew needed care and nourishment. I visited villages, pastors, homes. so, I could see their situations. And their everyday challenges.

The needs are great. But when I see these lovely, innocent children saved from misery and hunger I know it is worth all I can give. I cling to the promise that God will provide. And now I have seen the land that we are interested in buying and build our permanent home there. This is a huge faith challenge But with God on our side and with our dear friends, who partner with us, we will make a difference Orphans need safe hands Praise to be to our Lord Jesus We are trying to find the different projects to create a sustainable change Under safe hands children foundation and by October 2017 it started with the support from donors from different countries of the world until now.

Safe hands always welcome new opportunities in line with the organizational mission

I tell you, My Father in Heaven does not want one of these little children to be lost - Mathew 18:14
